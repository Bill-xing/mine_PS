My vision and robot-manipulation projects have given me a practical basis for linking perception to action. The unresolved gap is how to extend that experience from a fixed-view manipulator to the changing visual scene and sequential decisions of autonomous mobility. If the current curriculum continues into my intake, I would choose the Autonomous Driving stream in CityUHK's MSc in Artificial Intelligence to make that transfer explicit.

CS5187 Vision and Image, available as a Group II elective, would deepen how visual inputs are represented. SDSC6007 Dynamic Programming and Reinforcement Learning would then develop the sequential-decision layer, and CS5493 Topics in Autonomous Driving would place both within an autonomous-driving context. The six-credit CS6522 Project in Autonomous Driving is optional and mutually exclusive with the other experiential alternatives. If I selected that alternative and received approval, I would adapt a perception-to-action pipeline from robot manipulation to an autonomous-mobility task, vary visual uncertainty, and examine how it changes decisions and resulting control behavior. This transfer would prepare me to develop embodied systems across robotic manipulation and autonomous mobility.
