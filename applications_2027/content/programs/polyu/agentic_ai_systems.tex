Hermite taught me to examine how action representation constrains model outputs. In a separate project, the CR5/OpenPI loop gave me experience carrying policy chunks through WebSocket serving to robot execution. These are two endpoints of a larger systems question: how can an agent coordinate reasoning, model orchestration, cloud services, and physical action while keeping each transition inspectable? The MSc in Agentic AI Systems at The Hong Kong Polytechnic University (PolyU) targets this end-to-end systems problem.

The programme's emphasis on its "Zero-to-Physical Capstone: Bridging Software and Hardware" characteristic captures the direction I want to pursue. Where the listed subjects are offered, COMP5583 Agentic Computing, Systems, and Applications frames reasoning and orchestration; COMP5585 Cloud Computing and Hyperscale AI Infrastructure adds the cloud layer; and ME5301 Embodied Robot Intelligence carries the chain into physical sensing and control. I would bring stage-specific testing to this sequence: decision-output checks at the reasoning layer, service-response checks at the cloud boundary, and command checks at physical execution. Linking those tests through explicit interfaces would draw on distinct lessons from Hermite and CR5 and advance my goal of engineering dependable embodied agents for robotics R\&D.
