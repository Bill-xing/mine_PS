My Hermite work examines how an action representation retains trajectory structure, while CR5 deployment showed how new inference from an intermediate in-motion state under non-blocking execution could trigger unstable corrections. I have therefore studied representation and control as connected engineering layers, but I still need a rigorous way to validate their interaction with hardware limits and human collaboration. At The Hong Kong Polytechnic University (PolyU), the MSc in Intelligent Robotics Engineering would address this gap through an integrated view of learning, control, and robotic operation.

Among subjects available in my intake, Embodied Robot Intelligence connects learned action semantics to physical behavior; Advanced Control Technology deepens the feedback and stability reasoning behind my blocking short-horizon ServoP decision; and Industrial Human-Robot Systems and Automation brings human-aware workflows into a shared validation frame. If I were eligible for and selected the dissertation route, its seven taught subjects plus a Dissertation could sustain an investigation relating representation-fidelity tests to state-action consistency trials on hardware. My experience tracing failures across synchronized data, policy interfaces, and robot commands would give that work concrete starting cases. I aim to turn the resulting framework into dependable embodied systems for robotics R\&D.
