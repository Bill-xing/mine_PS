My Hermite work examines how an action representation retains trajectory structure, while CR5 deployment showed that execution timing can invalidate an otherwise coherent command. I have therefore studied representation and control as connected engineering layers, but I still need a rigorous way to validate their interaction with hardware limits and human collaboration. At The Hong Kong Polytechnic University (PolyU), the MSc in Intelligent Robotics Engineering would address this gap through an integrated view of learning, control, and robotic operation.

If the listed subjects are offered in my intake, Embodied Robot Intelligence would help me relate learned action semantics to physical behavior, while Advanced Control Technology would deepen the feedback and stability reasoning behind my blocking short-horizon ServoP decision. Industrial Human-Robot Systems and Automation would extend validation beyond an isolated robot toward interaction, workflow, and human-aware constraints. If I were eligible for and selected the dissertation route, its seven taught subjects plus a Dissertation could provide a sustained setting to investigate how representation fidelity and execution consistency should be tested together, without treating that route as guaranteed. I would bring experience tracing failures across synchronized data, policy interfaces, and hardware commands, and use it to develop dependable embodied systems for robotics R\&D.
