I am pursuing NTU's MSc in Computer Control & Automation because a real-robot deployment taught me that execution semantics determine whether a learned command remains meaningful. In an OpenPI loop for a Dobot CR5, I assembled RGB and robot-state observations, received action chunks through a WebSocket server, and translated them into ServoP arm targets and Modbus gripper commands. Under non-blocking operation, a new inference could consume an intermediate in-motion state before the previous chunk was complete, producing back-and-forth corrections. I adopted blocking, short-horizon execution, trading apparent frequency for state-action consistency. This solved the immediate problem but left me wanting the control theory to analyse it.

For the August 2027 intake, subject to course availability, EE6203 Computer Control Systems would help me examine computer-controlled dynamics instead of relying on one workaround. EE6225 Multivariable Control Systems Analysis & Design would deepen my understanding of coupled motion and feedback, while EE6221 Robotics & Intelligent Sensors would connect that analysis to the sensed state on which execution depends. These courses match the gap between building a functioning pipeline and explaining it systematically.
